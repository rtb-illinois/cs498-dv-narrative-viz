US House Voting Summary

The US House of Representatives is made up of 435 elected officials from different areas of the United States. Each state has at least 1 representative and the total number of representatives is based on the population of the state. That is, states with higher population (New York) have more representatives in the US House than states with lower population (Alaska). Voting for the US House happens every two years, more often than the US Presidency or the US Senate.

Elections for the US House also occur in sub-regions of a state, except for states with only one representative, in which case the election occurs across the entire state. The data from this visualization shows that ince 1976, the Democratic Party has held a majority in the US House slightly more often than the Republican Party. Because of the frequency of voting for US House Representatives, the controlling party also changes more often than for the US Senate or the US Presidency.
