US Presidency Voting Summary

Voting for the US Presidency happens every four years. Since 1976, the US Presidency has been occupied by a Republican slightly more often than it has been occupied by a Democrat. Republicans that have held this office include Ronald Reagan, George H. W. Bush, George W. Bush, and Donald Trump. Democrats that have occupied this office include Jimmy Carter, Bill Clinton, and Barack Obama.

While individual citizens all have the right to participate in elections for the US Presidency, the winner is ultimately determined by a structure called the Electoral College. One side effect of this is that a candidate does not necessarily need to win the most votes of individual citizens to be elected. For example, this happened in the 2016 election when Hillary Clinton won the popular vote, but Donald Trump won the electoral college vote and became the US President. The next presidential election will be held this fall in November 2020.
