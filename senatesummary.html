US Senate Voting Summary

The US Senate is made up of 100 elected officials from the 50 states. Each state elects two senators, regardless of the population of the state. So, populous states (e.g. New York) have the same amount of representation as less populated states (e.g. Alaska). These officials are elected via popular vote across the entirety of each state. Voting for senators only occurs once every six years, which is less often than voting for US House Representative and for the US Presidency.

The data from this visualization shows that since 1976, the Democratic Party has held a majority in the Senate slightly more often than the Republican Party. Because the US Senate is comprised of fewer members than the US House, the majority held is often only by a few votes. The data also shows that elections for the US Senate are often won by a more significant margin than other elections.
